About Jensen Woodworking

Custom woodwork crafted in coastal Massachusetts

I was introduced to woodworking in sixth grade in Denmark, where an elective course first taught me the satisfaction of shaping something useful by hand. After moving to the United States, I kept building whenever I could, from practical carpentry projects to furniture for family and friends.

After Tuck, I invested more seriously in the craft, inspired by makers like Norm Abram and drawn equally to design, precision, and the quiet rhythm of the shop. Over time, the work grew from personal projects into custom commissions for countertops, island tops, dining tables, and other pieces meant to be lived with every day.

Working from Duxbury's historic shipbuilding district feels especially fitting. The tradition of skilled making here, together with my love of honest materials and well-made details, shapes every project that leaves the shop.

I work exclusively on custom commissions and would be glad to talk through an idea with you.

About Klaus Jensen

30 years of care, patience, and doing things properly

I've been working with wood for more than 30 years, building a practice around patience, proportion, and doing things properly. I'm drawn to pieces that feel quiet but confident and grow more meaningful with daily use.

My Danish background still guides the work: simple forms, honest materials, and a belief that function and beauty should belong together. I use both traditional hand tools and modern equipment, depending on what the piece asks for, and I am especially drawn to wood with a history, including reclaimed material and fallen trees.

From kitchen countertops and islands to dining tables, bartops, mantels, and one-off commissions, each project starts with a conversation and is built to feel natural in the space it joins.

If you have something in mind, I'd be glad to talk.
