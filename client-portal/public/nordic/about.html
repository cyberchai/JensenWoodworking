About Jensen Woodworking

Custom woodwork crafted in coastal Massachusetts

Handcrafted furniture made with quality and care

I am passionate about creating beautiful and functional pieces that will last for generations. I began woodworking 30 years ago when I started crafting furniture for family and friends. Today, I am inspired by the natural beauty of wood and the satisfaction of creating something that will be cherished for years to come. My workshop is located in the heart of the historic Shipbuilding District in Duxbury - the many shipyards employed some of the finest woodworkers and carpenters back in the day. I also love the story of each piece of wood and finding ways to repurpose fallen trees - giving them a new lease on life.

My process is rooted in traditional woodworking techniques and modern technology. I never get tired of a carefully honed blockplane peeling off perfect wood shavings. Or a perfectly cut piece of lumber using modern equipment. I start with carefully selected, sustainably sourced wood and use precision tools to create each piece. I believe that attention to detail and quality craftsmanship are essential to creating furniture that is both beautiful and functional. I focus primarily on kitchen countertops, bartops, mantels and dining tables and kitchen islands. But your imagination is my limit!

About Klaus Jensen

30 years of care, patience, and doing things properly

I've been working with wood for over 30 years, first making pieces for family and friends, and eventually building a practice around care, patience, and doing things properly. I'm drawn to work that feels quiet but confident—pieces that don't ask for attention, but earn it over time.

My Danish background has always shaped how I think about making things: simple forms, honest materials, and a belief that function and beauty should never compete. That sensibility carries through everything I build. I also bring the discipline of an Ivy League education into the shop—an attention to detail, proportion, and process that shows up in the finished work, even when it isn't immediately visible.

My workshop sits in Duxbury's historic shipbuilding district, where skilled carpenters once built vessels meant to last at sea. Working here keeps me connected to that tradition. I'm especially drawn to wood with a history—fallen trees, reclaimed material—giving it a second life with intention.

I use a mix of traditional hand tools and modern equipment. There's nothing better than a sharp block plane or a perfectly cut joint. I focus mainly on kitchen countertops, dining tables, islands, bartops, and mantels, but many projects start simply with a conversation.

If you have something in mind, I'd be glad to talk.
